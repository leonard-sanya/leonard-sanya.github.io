WELCOME!, I'm Leonard Sanya

BIO

When I was growing up, I was the kind of kid who found patterns in everything — from the layout of stars in the night sky to the symmetry in school equations. Mathematics wasn’t just a subject for me; it was a language I felt naturally fluent in. This quiet obsession eventually led me to Kibabii University, where I enrolled in a Bachelor of Science in Mathematics. By my third year, I had chosen to specialize in Applied Mathematics, eager to understand how math could be used to solve problems beyond the classroom. But when I graduated, I hit a wall.

I had all this theoretical knowledge, yet no clear direction on how it translated into real-world impact. That uncertainty was difficult — until I received a scholarship from the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS). AIMS became a turning point. It was there that I was introduced to the worlds of data science and machine learning — disciplines that made mathematics come alive in ways I hadn’t imagined. I was suddenly surrounded by people who used math not just to solve equations, but to tackle real, tangible problems — from climate modeling to public health, from financial forecasting to natural language understanding.

For the first time, I saw how the abstract tools I had studied could be used to make sense of the messy, complex world we live in. The confusion I once had gave way to clarity and purpose. Today, I continue that journey — exploring, building, and learning. I'm driven by curiosity and a quiet belief that mathematics, when paired with the right tools and mindset, can help solve some of the most important challenges of our time.
